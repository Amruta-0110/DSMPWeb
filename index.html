Basic Terms in Indian Stock Markets

High

The highest price at which a stock traded during the trading day or a given period.

Low

The lowest price at which a stock traded during the trading day or a given period.

Open

The price at which a stock first trades upon the opening of an exchange on a trading day.

Close

The last price at which a stock trades during a regular trading session.

Volume

The total number of shares or contracts traded in a security or market during a given period.

52 Weeks High

The highest price at which a stock has traded during the last 52 weeks.

52 Weeks Low

The lowest price at which a stock has traded during the last 52 weeks.

Nifty

The Nifty, or the Nifty 50, is the flagship index on the National Stock Exchange of India, which tracks 50 major stocks across 13 sectors.

Demat Account

A dematerialized account used to hold shares and securities in electronic format, facilitating easy trade for the users.

Bull Market

A market condition where prices are rising or are expected to rise. The term is typically used in reference to the stock market, but can be applied to anything that is traded, such as bonds, currencies, and commodities.

Bear Market

A market condition where prices are falling or are expected to fall. This term is often used in the stock market and can be seen as the opposite of a bull market.

IPO (Initial Public Offering)

An IPO is the process through which a private company can go public by sale of its stocks to the general public. It could be a new, young company or an old company which decides to be listed on an exchange and hence goes public.
